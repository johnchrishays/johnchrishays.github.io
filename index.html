Chris Hays

Hello! I'm a Research Affiliate of the Computation and Society Initiative at Yale. I recently graduated from Yale with a degree in computer science.

With the Computation and Society Initiative, I am advised by Nisheeth Vishnoi (Yale Computer Science) and Elisa Celis (Yale Statistics and Data Science) on a project studying mechanisms for hiring fairness.

Research Interests

I am interested in questions at the intersection of computer science and issues affecting society.

At Yale, I particularly enjoyed my courses in optimization and spectral graph theory as well as constitutional law and the history of economic development targeted at urban poverty.

Writing

I am very excited about the Computation and Society blog that I helped start and to which I regularly contribute. The blog addresses foundational questions that arise as algorithms and data-driven technologies permeate and alter various aspects of modern society.

I have also written about detecting deepfakes using transformers, left-leaning faith-based activism in New Haven, and Ben Carson's time at Yale.
